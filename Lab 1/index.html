What is a Wireless Sensor Network?
"Consists of spatially distributed autonomous sensors to cooperatively monitor physical or environmental conditions" -Wikipedia

The figure above depicts an example of what a Wireless Sensor Network (WSN) is capable of. The WSN consists of a group of Wireless 'Nodes (shown right) that all relay data to the main 'Host.' A group of nodes form a Wireless Sensor Network.
